Trigonometry

Basic Definitions (Right-Angled Triangle)

For a right-angled triangle with angle θ, the three sides are the hypotenuse (opposite the right angle), the opposite side (across from θ), and the adjacent side (next to θ).

sin(θ) = Opposite / Hypotenuse

cos(θ) = Adjacent / Hypotenuse

tan(θ) = Opposite / Adjacent = sin(θ)/cos(θ)

The Unit Circle

The unit circle has radius 1 centered at the origin. For any angle θ, the point on the unit circle is (cos θ, sin θ). This extends trig functions beyond 0°–90° to all angles.

Signs by quadrant (CAST rule):

  • Quadrant I (0°–90°): All positive
  • Quadrant II (90°–180°): Sin positive only
  • Quadrant III (180°–270°): Tan positive only
  • Quadrant IV (270°–360°): Cos positive only

Trigonometric Identities

Pythagorean Identity:

sin²(θ) + cos²(θ) = 1

Derived forms:

1 + tan²(θ) = sec²(θ)

1 + cot²(θ) = cosec²(θ)

Double Angle Formulas:

sin(2θ) = 2 sin(θ) cos(θ)

cos(2θ) = cos²(θ) - sin²(θ)

Triangle Congruence

Two triangles are congruent (identical in shape and size) if any of the following conditions hold:

Congruence Conditions

  • SSS: All three sides are equal
  • SAS: Two sides and the included angle are equal
  • ASA: Two angles and the included side are equal
  • RHS: Right angle, hypotenuse, and one side are equal

Triangle Similarity

Two triangles are similar (same shape, different size) if corresponding angles are equal. Their sides are in the same ratio.

Similarity Conditions

  • AA: Two pairs of equal angles (the third must also match)
  • SAS Similarity: Two sides in the same ratio with equal included angle
  • SSS Similarity: All three sides in the same ratio

Key consequence of similarity: If two triangles are similar with scale factor k, their areas are in the ratio k².

Sine and Cosine Rules

Sine Rule:

a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)

Cosine Rule:

a² = b² + c² - 2bc cos(A)

Use sine rule when you have a side-angle pair. Use cosine rule when you have three sides or two sides + included angle.

Example: Sine Rule

In triangle ABC, angle A = 40°, angle B = 60°, side a = 10. Find side b.

b/sin(60°) = 10/sin(40°)

b = 10 × sin(60°)/sin(40°) ≈ 10 × 0.866/0.643 ≈ 13.5
