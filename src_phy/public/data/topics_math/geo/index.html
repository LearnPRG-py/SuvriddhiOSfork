Geometry

Lines and Angles

Key Angle Types:

  • Acute: less than 90°
  • Right: exactly 90°
  • Obtuse: between 90° and 180°
  • Reflex: between 180° and 360°
  • Supplementary angles: add to 180°
  • Complementary angles: add to 90°

Angle Rules with Parallel Lines

  • Corresponding angles: equal (same position at each intersection)
  • Alternate angles: equal (Z-shape)
  • Co-interior angles: add to 180° (C-shape)
  • Vertically opposite angles: always equal

Polygons

A polygon is a closed 2D shape with straight sides. The sum of interior angles depends on the number of sides.

Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon:

(n - 2) × 180°

Triangle: 180° | Quadrilateral: 360° | Pentagon: 540° | Hexagon: 720°

Regular polygons: all sides and angles equal. Each interior angle = (n-2) × 180° / n

Area and Perimeter of Standard Shapes

Rectangle: Area = l × w  |  Perimeter = 2(l + w)

Parallelogram: Area = base × height

Trapezium: Area = ½ × (a + b) × h  (a, b = parallel sides)

Circle: Area = πr²  |  Circumference = 2πr

Sector of circle: Area = (θ/360) × πr²  |  Arc length = (θ/360) × 2πr

Circle Theorems

These are key results about angles and lines in circles that you should know well.

The Main Circle Theorems

  • Angle at centre: The angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference subtended by the same arc.
  • Angle in semicircle: The angle in a semicircle (subtended by a diameter) is always 90°.
  • Angles in same segment: Angles subtended by the same chord on the same side are equal.
  • Cyclic quadrilateral: Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral add to 180°.
  • Tangent-radius: A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.
  • Tangent from external point: Two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length.
  • Alternate segment theorem: The angle between a tangent and a chord equals the inscribed angle on the opposite side.

Example: Angle at Centre

If the angle subtended at the circumference by an arc is 35°, what is the angle at the centre?

Angle at centre = 2 × 35° = 70°

3D Shapes: Surface Area and Volume

Cuboid: Volume = l × w × h  |  Surface Area = 2(lw + lh + wh)

Cylinder: Volume = πr²h  |  Surface Area = 2πr² + 2πrh

Sphere: Volume = (4/3)πr³  |  Surface Area = 4πr²

Cone: Volume = (1/3)πr²h  |  Slant height l = √(r² + h²)
